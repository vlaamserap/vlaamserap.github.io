V/A – Networking vol. I

1 april 2007

Gratis Download! – This album is the result of a project I started on in September 2004. Paul Van Bruystegem, a famous session guitarist and co-owner of Redtape Studios, needed me to lay down some cuts for an experimental cd he wanted to put out. In exchange he offered me some recording time, which I considered a good deal, since there were plenty of collabos I could record. At first we started off by recording with the Riot Squad mc’s, my longtime crew. But as we went on, I started playing with the idea of recording a full album there, with guest rappers and singers from all over the globe. So I networked. I hooked up with all the artists I knew and appreciated, and checked who wanted to hook up and do a track. I contacted people I’d already worked with, but also people whose music I dig. After a few months, we had already recorded numerous tracks, and more were on the way. But then the owner of the building where Redtape was located (Meerhout) decided to evict them, and they were forced to clear out the studio in no time. This left me with about 17 raw tracks, unmixed and unedited.
  

The studio would just move to another location, and we planned to pick up where we left off with mixing, but everything took so much time I just kept sending out tracks to artists and tried to find other ways to record. Eventually, it became clear that the Redtape crew was so consumed with building their new studio that it’d be better for me to find an alternative to finish the Networking project.

So I hooked up with my main man Juz from Bloombury, who has a full Protools Digi002 setup in his home studio. He was down to mix the whole thing, so we started from scratch there. By the time we actually began mixing (it was early 2006 by then), he had a mother board crash, and his PC was in reparation for about a month. So obviously, this album has been in the making for more than two years now. At times I just wanted to drop the whole project, but then again, that would be a waste of time and good music. So here we are, it’s April 2007, and after having to tell people time and time again that there would be a little delay on the release, I’m finally able to put out all these tracks that have been sitting on the shelf for a while now. But..

..the music industry today is not what it was in 2004; people just seemed to stop buying cd’s and vinyl, and downloading (legally and illegally) has made the market crash. Releasing a cd nowadays is a risk in its own, especially when it concerns underground music. Even though there was interest from a few small labels – no deals were closed or nothing – I probably would have release the cd myself. But as it stands, I don’t have the financial means to take this risk, so I decided to put the Networking album online for free. It’s not that I’m afraid of downloading, I just realise how many more people you can reach when your music is available legally on the internet.

So there you go.. Networking, literally. Go to the download page to listen to snippets from all 22 tracks or download the zipfile containing the full album and artwork.

Damented aka D Minor 7
